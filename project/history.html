The Beginning of the Edmonton Oilers

In 1972, The Oilers were formed as one of 12 teams in the newly established professional Hockey league, The World Hockey Association (WHA). The team was originally placed in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada, but started out with the official name, "The Alberta Oilers". Due to competition with the already established National Hockey League (NHL), it didn't take long to see profits for the WHA considered unremarkable, compared to it's competitor.

-

In just 7 years, The WHA agreed to merge with the NHL, allowing the NHL to expand with some of the teams from the the WHA. The Oilers was one of the few franchises that joined the NHL, now having changed their name to the Edmonton Oilers, and starting a feud with rival Alberta team, the Calgary Flames.

The Original Ownership of the Oilers

The original owners of the franchise were Bill and Daryl Hunter. Bill was a hockey player and promoter for the sport. He helped invest in the World Hockey Association, and helped start a franchise in Edmonton. He and his son, Darryl, were responsible for the team's name. They chose the Oilers because Alberta's economy was reliant on Oil from the province's vast resource in petroleum.
